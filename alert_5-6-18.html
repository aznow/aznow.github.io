SOS FOR SCHOOL FUNDING

The budget and school funding were the main issues to resolve this week. The teacher walk out that started on Thursday April 26 continues to be very inspiring.

Once again this week, the Senate cut and ran on Wed. noon. They returned later and "worked" until 5:30 a.m. on Thursday to pass the education portion of the budget. The teachers likewise stayed through the night - in the gallery and out on the lawn. All the amendments put forward by the Democrats as requested by the teachers were voted down: 25/classroom, counselor 250/1 ratio, for support staff. The Republican budget passed and Ducey signed it.

Though the teachers did not get all they wanted, they got a lot. The budget started out with $65 million for education and ended up with $400 million. This proves what people power can do.

The citizens initiative "Invest in Education" that would increase taxes on those making more than $250,000 per year, would bring in $700 million and with that, education funding would be back to where it was in 2008. But 10 years have passed. Technology has changed, busses have more miles, schools have gotten older, cost of living has gone up, books have become outdated, and teachers and support staff need raises. So restoring education to 2008 levels is not enough.

In the midst of all this, to prove the complete tone deafness of our legislature, they are trying to pass more vouchers to siphon more tax money out of public schools into for-profit schools - often ones they own.

Some "big lies" have been told e.g. that this budget will provide $600 million over 3 years. It's a one year budget. It still relies on rosy economic projections. It still sweeps money from what was intended for highway funds. House members have said that putting $1 billion into education is a historic investment in Arizona. Not true. In the last 10 years, the legislature stole $1 billion from education so putting it back just restores education to 2008 levels. Putting back what you stole is not an investment! It means you got caught. We need that and more.

The citizens initiative is a good start. Let's all get busy getting those signatures. Contact David Lujan at Arizona Center for Economic Progress at 602-266-0707.

Don't be fooled by Ducey's upcoming $3M ad campaign, which is aimed to convince you his plan works. It hasn't worked and it won't work for Arizona unless we fully fund public education. Remember in November.
